Hi, my love.
This is my way of writing a letter for you.
I know the last couple of months have been hard for us, and I also know you have many things going on with university.
I just want to say a few things, my life. Thank you for being in my life.
I know we weren’t expecting to meet each other, but now I can say it’s the best thing that has ever happened to me.
You are the sweetest, kindest, smartest, and most beautiful person.
Every day I thank God for your life.
I remember everything: our first kiss and the first time we held hands —
those were some of the most beautiful moments of our relationship.
Since the first day we thought about starting our relationship, I have been so happy.
I can no longer imagine my life without you.
And I certainly cannot imagine it without
the absurd expenses, without cleaning ovens,
and other small annoyances that, of course,
I am willing to accept.
I look at you and reflect: long-term love is much deeper and more meaningful than the passion of a relationship’s early days.
To transcend the novelty without losing desire, patience, and affection.
That is what I have always wanted for my love life — to face hard times together,
to fight routine with noble arguments (but without harshness), and to respect each other’s individual freedom.
I look at you and conclude: I hope you sleep by my side for the rest of my life.
